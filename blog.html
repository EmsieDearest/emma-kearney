Episode 2

You know that scene in Fight Club when Edward Norton's character blows up his apartment because none of it mattered to him? He was so numb that he wanted to feel something real after years of a meaningless 9-5 existence. I did something similar once when I was going through a rough breakup a few years ago and channelled that turbulent creative energy into transforming my studio apartment into a wacky combination of Willy Wonka's chocolate factory meets Matisse meets a 1960s nightclub. (10 out of 10 would do again.) Then I quit my job with no prospects (1 out of 10 would not do again), joined a feminist social club, grew my hair out super long, worked my butt off and diligently built up a career that I'm now very proud of.

It just made me wonder how people come up with their best ideas. What state of mind were they in? Some created out of necessity like Kurt Cobain and JK Rowling. Some were motivated by breaking down conventions like Frank Lloyd Wright. Hayao Miyazaki was inspired by themes of natural beauty and the relationship between environment and humanity--a contrast from the futuristic themes of manga. Weird Al wanted to make polka music cool but his dad thought that was a pointless career and made him go to architecture school instead. He always kept the dream alive until he got his big break.

So the 9-5 has forever changed with the world on pause. I've reconnected with my child-like sense of wonder. Filling the void with magic and fun. I can't wait to see all of the creativity that has spawned from the silence of 2020. I think we're on the verge of something big. Either that or we're getting another Full House reunion. I wouldn't hate it.





Episode 1

Hi. I'm Emma. Wow introductions are hard. I've just been sitting here at my desk looking around the room hoping something will spark a conversation starter. And for the 4th time I'm looking at my Dungeons & Dragons player handbook and my mind has suppressed the urge to discuss in full detail the reasons why paladins make highly underrated characters. No Emma, we should not make this everyone's first impression of you. Oops...

Well I guess you were going to find out soon enough. I'm a nerd. A huge nerd. I'm a nerd's nerd. I'm nerdy about weird cool things such as above. I'm a design nerd. A music nerd. A culture nerd. I have whole scripts memorized of movies that are not very good and make me very happy nonetheless.

I don't really have a goal for this blog. There are literally 1 billion kajillion blogs out there (I counted) and mine surely will not make the top ten. I have a feeling that it may surpass the bottom 10--it's important to make achievable goals. My Dad (you may call him Papa K) and I enlist in a canoe race every year and we strive to come in dead last. It's a tough commitment. We take lots of breaks. We fish. We may be hungry (good thing we brought these sandwich fixings) or use the rest room (the world is your rest room), but alas, we stick to our goal. We are proud final finishers 5 years in a row.

Every blog and their mother shares recipes of their favorite dishes. What about a recipe of a good time? You don't need to buy anything or go anywhere. All you need is a piece of paper. You won't know what you're making or if you're doing it right until the end. That's part of the fun!

  1. Cut or tear down to a square size.
  2. Fold one corner to the opposite corner.
  3. Then take the two corners on the long side and bring them to the center corner.
  4. Now it's a square. Flip paper over.
  5. Take the left and right corners and fold them so that the edges align into the center.
  6. Take a loose bottom corner and fold upwards as far as it will go.
  7. Flip over. Fold the bottom tips upwards as far as they will go.
  8. Now take those tips you just folded upwards and fold them outward halfway on both sides.
  9. Now mark 1/3rd of the way down of the bottom lip. Fold the bottom corner so that the bottom edge sits at that mark.
  10. Now fold that last bit upwards as far as it will go.


It's a party for one complete with a cool party hat! Optional extra step: feather or bedazzle to your hearts content. Here's a fun playlist I made to kickstart this cool dance party. Enjoy!
