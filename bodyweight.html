Bodyweight

For most people, exercising is not fun. Most people do not wake up first thing in the morning looking forward to an hour of cardio or strength training. It's a means to an end. Exercising at home isn't as motivating as going to a class and keeping up with the group. Sure, it's more convenient. But for many, it can be uninspiring. Users want streaming workouts that are fun, challenging, inclusive and affordable. Like a trendy studio, only completely online and an empowering community to boot. One of the nice things about using an online service for exercise is that you have access to instructors located in more than one market. Someone living in Kansas City could take a yoga class from a popular instructor based out of NYC or LA for a fraction of the normal cost.

The history of the workout studio is like a great coming of age story. Gyms used to be a standard four white walls and a few weight machines. They were used primarily by athletic programs and body builders. Then the big hair and neon leotards of the 80s made the gym mainstream. They became cool places to hang out with upbeat music to pump you up, instructors with big personalities and everyone dressed in funky outfits. Jane Fonda and Richard Simmons had amassed cult followings lasting decades. It was so much more than a workout. It was a lifestyle and a supportive community.

The goal was to bring a resurgence of the fitness community and lifestyle to a streaming service. Each interaction needed to convey a positive emotion. The process beginning from signing in to joining a class needed to feel exciting. Being in the class needed to feel like a shared experience with others. Even though users couldn't see other participants in the class, they needed to feel like they are in sync with a hundred others across the country. Special events would drive member engagement. Being able to follow instructors on social media would allow users to get to know each other on a more personal level. Social media could also be a place to rep store merchandise and build brand awareness.
