Bodyweight

PROBLEM

People stuck at home are also working out from home. There are so many ways to find a workout online, whether through YouTube or another app. There are other streaming services, gyms converting their in-person training to virtual. There's really no excuse not to make time to exercise with all of the options and convenience. Except for one thing. Motivation. Sure, it's so much more convenient now, but for many, it's a drag to track down good workouts.

This app is for users who want a workout that is fun, doesn't require buying new equipment, relies on your own body weight and challenging enough to see progress. Using the app should hype users up so they feel pumped and ready to break a sweat once they decide on a class. It should feel empowering and like they are part of a community that values growth, self-love and connection.

IDEATION

The rapid sticky-note ideation process went something like this. What makes an app fun? Some popular fun apps are TikTok, Snapchat, Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube. What makes these fun? The social aspect, they make you feel cooler. The content is world class. Which apps support self-love? Headspace, Calm, Flo, Youper, Sanity&Self. Why? They use bright or calming colors. The content is thoughtful. They utilize AI that makes you feel seen. Helps you learn something about yourself. What about brands with cult followings? Nike, Apple, Supreme, SoHo House. These brands are not just about the product. It's a lifestyle. It's how they market themselves. Which ones make you feel connected to others? Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, Messenger, Eventbrite, Slack, Discord. You get a glimpse into the lives of people. You can chat with others.

How can we differentiate ourselves from the hoards of other options? We motivate the user with highly entertaining workouts they keep coming back to. Bringing back the excitement Richard Simmons brought to every workout. We can make it fun by having really cool instructors. Some are regular instructors who bring a lot of fun. Then there are guests like professional ballet dancers, renowned instructors or athletes who are sponsored by the app. We make them accessible with live or on-demand classes. They can see who is tuning in and will call users names out throughout the workout.

How will it function? The classes should be available on both desktop and mobile. Can this be available on Apple TV? Will this be subscription-based or pay-per-class? Maybe to be even more inclusive, we can have free users choose one class per week. Pay by class could get regular classes minus special events. Subscribers get access to the whole shebang plus discounts in the merch store. Users need to be able to quickly pick a class to stream live or replay. Should these be on the same screen or different ones? It would be helpful to see the full list of these classes offered that day. Users can choose to add it to their calendar or save it for later. Can the user see more detail about the class before joining it? Will users be able to see which classes will be offered that week? Month? Can users be notified in advance of a special class or event? Can there be a post-workout after party for special classes? Maybe a Q&A? A chat room? Maybe subscribers could have access to a virtual "gym lounge" for networking. What other incentives could we add? Should this be introduced at a later phase to create some buzz? Which areas will give us the biggest impact first and foremost?

SOLUTION

With an endless array of ideas flowing, it was time to get organized. What could we implement first? We thought it was important to be inclusive. Free users could dip their toe in the water with access to one class a month. Subscribers would pay by the month or year. We needed a few great instructors to get us going. A schedule that changes everyday of live classes. Popular classes will be selected for replay. Depending on how well the first phase goes, we can enter into phase two with special guests and merchandise. Phase three could include the "gym lounge" for virtual retreats and networking.
