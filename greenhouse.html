Greenhouse App

PROBLEM

People stuck at home are taking up DIY hobbies more than ever with indoor gardening taking over Instagram's feed. Lovely photos of succulents and cacti in funky planters, vines outlining curated bookshelves and trendy swiss cheese plants galore. This app is for Instagrammers who love the idea of owning a bunch of cool looking plants but often forget to care for them. There were many concerns about how this app would function.



IDEATION

There needed to be an easy way to catalog all of the plants the user owns. What happens when they try to catalog a plant that isn't in the system? Can they add a new one? And once the plants are all cataloged, the user needs to be notified of a reminder on their phone. Does this need permission? Can the user adjust those settings? Can we include other reminders such as when a plant is due for fertilization and re-potting? To keep track of all of this information, the user would need to set up an account. Could we apply identity correlation within our login process? What other features could we include? Could there be a plant genesis algorithm where you can discover other plants based on the ones the user has cataloged? Or could there be a steady stream of content creation in a "discover" section? Could there be a community section where users could share photos of the state of their plant and ask if it's worth saving? Or maybe there could be a tips and tricks section implemented into each cataloged plant?

SOLUTION

To solve these problems, I used Adobe InDesign for organizing the User Flow. The user would set up an account or sign in. Once the account has been created and the user can sign in, the user would be guided through a series of next steps. The first, adding their plants into a catalog. Next, asking for permission to send reminders. Once those initial steps are completed, the user will be directed to the dashboard where they can explore other features such as the discover section where they can learn about other plants and the community section. The user can also see the plants in their catalog if they go into their account and read all of the tips and tricks.

I first sketched some ideas for wireframes and brought them into Adobe XD for further exploration and interactive prototyping. I designed the rest of the UI with a combination of Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and XD. I took the final design into Adobe After Effects to create a high fidelity prototype you see below. This app is currently in development.
