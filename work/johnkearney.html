Art of Accessibility

Our society has a fascination with artists after they pass away. Their soul becomes immortal in the legacy of their work. Their memory lives on in the stories retold. John Kearney was a great American artist who grew up in poverty and abuse and instilled fun, festivity and playfulness into everything he did. He was so much more than the infamous found chrome bumpers he sculpted into life-sized animals. The John Kearney Family Estate wanted to share this amazing story and perhaps inspire those to pursue their dreams no matter who they are or where they come from. He wanted his art to be available to everyone. This virtual museum is designed and programmed to be accessibility compliant, inclusive and interactive. It is a time capsule celebrating the artist and person that was John Kearney.

In this massive project, I uncovered negatives that had never seen the light of day. I digitally archived records and hundreds of photographs collected by his late wife over the course of his 40-year career. I cross-referenced information from The Smithsonian, Chicago History Museum, Berta Walker Gallery, and close friends and associates. It also helped that John Kearney was my Grandfather. I was up for this labor of love.
